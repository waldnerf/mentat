# Mentat Knowledge Governance v1.0

## Purpose

Knowledge governance defines how Mentat maintains quality, coherence and long-term value.

Governance ensures that knowledge remains:

- understandable
- connected
- reusable
- trustworthy
- maintainable

Governance is not bureaucracy.

It is stewardship of the knowledge system.

---

# 1. Canonical Knowledge Principle

Every important concept should have one canonical Markdown source.

The canonical asset contains the durable understanding.

Artefacts are generated expressions.

Examples:

## Canonical Knowledge

- principles
- frameworks
- models
- concepts
- specifications
- decision records

## Generated Artefacts

- website pages
- presentations
- diagrams
- social posts
- documentation

Rule:

> Maintain the knowledge. Regenerate the artefacts.

Do not manually maintain duplicate sources.

---

# 2. Markdown as the Source of Truth

Markdown is the default knowledge format.

It provides:

- portability
- transparency
- version history
- collaboration
- AI compatibility

Knowledge should remain accessible without requiring a specific platform.

---

# 3. Knowledge Locations

Mentat separates knowledge by maturity.

## Inbox

Purpose:

Capture ideas.

Location:


knowledge/inbox/


Characteristics:

- incomplete
- unstructured
- exploratory

---

## Explorations

Purpose:

Develop ideas.

Location:


knowledge/explorations/


Characteristics:

- structured thinking
- hypotheses
- early models

---

## Assets

Purpose:

Store reusable knowledge.

Location:


knowledge/assets/


Examples:


knowledge/assets/principles/
knowledge/assets/frameworks/
knowledge/assets/models/
knowledge/assets/concepts/


Characteristics:

- refined
- connected
- reusable

---

# 4. Knowledge Ownership

Every important asset should have clear ownership.

Ownership means responsibility for:

- accuracy
- relevance
- evolution
- connections

Ownership does not restrict collaboration.

---

# 5. Knowledge Relationships

Knowledge increases in value through connections.

Mature assets should identify:

- related concepts
- dependencies
- extensions
- examples
- applications

Avoid isolated knowledge islands.

---

# 6. Decision Memory

Important decisions should preserve reasoning.

Capture:

## Decision

What was decided?

## Context

Why was the decision required?

## Alternatives

What options were considered?

## Rationale

Why was this approach selected?

## Consequences

What changes because of this decision?

---

# 7. Knowledge Debt

Knowledge systems accumulate debt.

Examples:

- duplicate concepts
- conflicting definitions
- outdated guidance
- disconnected assets
- unclear naming
- unused structures

Knowledge debt reduces system value.

Regularly identify and reduce it.

---

# 8. Terminology Governance

Mentat should maintain consistent language.

Important terms should have clear definitions.

Maintain:


knowledge/assets/concepts/


or:


knowledge/glossary/


Avoid unnecessary variation in terminology.

---

# 9. Versioning

Knowledge evolves.

Changes should preserve history.

Important changes should explain:

- what changed
- why it changed
- what impact it has

Prefer evolution over replacement.

---

# 10. Review Process

Review assets based on value, not age.

Ask:

## Clarity

Can someone understand this quickly?

## Accuracy

Is this still correct?

## Relevance

Does this still matter?

## Reuse

Can this generate new value?

## Connection

Is it linked to related knowledge?

---

# 11. Archiving

Archive knowledge when it becomes:

- obsolete
- superseded
- no longer relevant

Preserve history where useful.

Removing noise improves signal.

---

# Governance Principle

The purpose of governance is to make knowledge compound.

A healthy Mentat system becomes:

- easier to understand
- easier to extend
- easier to reuse
- more valuable over time.
