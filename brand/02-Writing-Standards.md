# Mentat Writing Standards v1.0

## Purpose

Mentat communication should reflect structured thinking, technical depth and practical experience.

The writing style combines:

- strategy consulting discipline
- engineering precision
- clear teaching
- practical application

The objective is not to produce more words.

The objective is to create understanding.

---

# Core Writing Principles

## 1. Lead With the Answer

Start with the conclusion.

The reader should understand the main point immediately.

Preferred structure:

1. conclusion
2. rationale
3. evidence
4. details

Do not make readers discover the message.

---

## 2. Communicate Top-Down

Organise information from highest value to lowest detail.

Preferred structure:


Key message

↓

Supporting arguments

↓

Evidence

↓

Details


---

## 3. Structure Before Expression

Before writing:

- define the objective
- identify the audience
- determine the key message
- select the appropriate structure

Writing is the expression of thinking.

---

## 4. Prefer Frameworks Over Prose

When explaining complex ideas, use:

- frameworks
- models
- taxonomies
- decision trees
- architectures
- tables
- diagrams

A good framework creates reusable understanding.

---

## 5. Apply MECE Thinking

Use MECE principles where practical.

## Mutually Exclusive

Avoid unnecessary overlap.

## Collectively Exhaustive

Cover the important dimensions.

The goal is clarity, not artificial structure.

---

# Language Standards

## Use

- short sentences
- precise terminology
- active voice
- concrete examples
- clear definitions

---

## Prefer

Instead of describing what something is not, describe what it is.

Example:

Avoid:

"This is not a document repository."

Prefer:

"This is a knowledge operating system."

---

# Tone

Mentat writing should be:

## Confident

Present clear points of view.

## Calm

Avoid hype and urgency.

## Analytical

Explain reasoning.

## Practical

Connect ideas to application.

## Human

Maintain natural communication.

---

# Avoid

Do not use:

- filler
- exaggerated claims
- generic statements
- unnecessary adjectives
- marketing language without substance

Avoid generic AI phrasing:

- "In today's rapidly changing world"
- "unlock potential"
- "game changer"
- "cutting-edge"
- "revolutionary"

---

# Punctuation Rules

Do not use em dashes.

Prefer:

- periods
- commas
- colons
- parentheses where appropriate

---

# Formatting Principles

Prefer:

- clear headings
- numbered structures
- bullet points
- tables
- diagrams

Avoid:

- long unbroken paragraphs
- unnecessary introductions
- repeated conclusions

---

# Visual Communication

Use visuals when they improve understanding.

Prefer:

- diagrams
- models
- architectures
- process flows
- comparison tables

Every visual should communicate meaning.

---

# Reuse Principle

Written assets should be designed for transformation.

A strong knowledge asset can become:

- an article
- a presentation
- a carousel
- a talk
- documentation
- a workshop

Create knowledge assets, not isolated content.

---

# Final Quality Check

Before publishing:

## Clarity

Is the message immediately understandable?

## Structure

Does the logic flow?

## Value

Does this create useful knowledge?

## Reuse

Can this become another artefact?

## Consistency

Does this follow Mentat standards?

---

# Writing Principle

Clear thinking creates clear writing.
Structure creates understanding.
Understanding creates reusable knowledge.
