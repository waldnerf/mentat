## Slide 2, The shift (light)

Eyebrow: The shift

Headline: Redesigning workflows does not compound

Body: AI delivers value by designing new processes and reorganising work around them. Disconnected tools mushroom. Each is built for a task, not for the people using it. Most efforts stall here: processes proliferate, none designed for the workforce, and adoption is left to chance.  The real question is release: how do these new processes reach the people who run them?

## Slide 3, The model (light)

Eyebrow: The model

Headline: An AI product is the intelligent front door to agentic processes

Body: One coherent entry point that orchestrates your agentic workflows. The design starts with the workforce: how people enter it, interact with it, and come to trust it. Cognitive offload, stress reduction and satisfaction are built in alongside productivity, so adoption feels natural. By crafting the experience, the new way of working is adopted more easily

## Slide 4, The framework (light)

Eyebrow: The framework

Headline: Five dimensions shape the experience

Dimensions:

1. Scope. Which processes, and which personae, is it built for?
2. Access. Does it live in a dedicated interface, or embedded where people already work?
3. Interaction. Is it proactive or reactive, and does it serve both the doer and the supervisor?
4. Judgment. Does it show its work, and know when to hand off to a human?
5. Memory. Does it carry context across sessions, and does correction improve it over time?

Note: Scope comes first. It shapes every answer after it
