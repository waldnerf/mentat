# Mentat Knowledge Quality Standards v1.0

## Purpose

Knowledge quality standards define the criteria for creating, maintaining and promoting Mentat knowledge assets.

The objective is not to maximise the quantity of knowledge.

The objective is to maximise the value, clarity and reuse of knowledge.

A high-quality asset strengthens the overall system.

---

# Quality Principles

## 1. Clarity

Knowledge should be understandable quickly.

A reader should understand:

- what this is
- why it matters
- how it can be used

Avoid unnecessary complexity.

---

## 2. Structure

Knowledge should have explicit structure.

Prefer:

- frameworks
- models
- principles
- taxonomies
- decision trees
- architectures

Structure makes knowledge reusable.

---

## 3. Precision

Use accurate terminology.

Define important concepts.

Avoid ambiguity.

Prefer specific language over broad statements.

---

## 4. Relevance

Every knowledge asset should have a purpose.

Clarify:

- who needs this
- what problem it addresses
- what decision it supports
- what action it enables

---

## 5. Reusability

Strong knowledge assets create value beyond their original context.

A reusable asset can become:

- an article
- a presentation
- a diagram
- a workshop
- documentation
- a product feature

---

## 6. Connection

Knowledge should exist within a network.

A mature asset identifies:

- related concepts
- dependencies
- extensions
- applications

---

# Knowledge Asset Requirements

Before promoting an exploration into a knowledge asset, it should contain:

## Purpose

Why does this exist?

---

## Context

What situation does it address?

---

## Core Idea

What is the essential insight?

---

## Structure

How is the idea organised?

---

## Application

How can it be used?

---

## Examples

Where does it apply?

---

## Relationships

What existing knowledge does it connect to?

---

## Evolution Notes

How might it improve?

---

# Writing Quality Standard

Mentat writing should:

- start with the conclusion
- communicate top-down
- use clear structures
- prefer frameworks over long explanations
- use precise language
- remove unnecessary complexity

---

# Avoid

Avoid:

- generic introductions
- filler statements
- exaggerated claims
- unnecessary adjectives
- repeated conclusions
- vague terminology
- artificial AI-style language

---

# Framework Quality Standard

A framework should:

- solve a clear problem
- have defined components
- explain relationships
- support decisions
- be applicable in practice

Prefer simple frameworks that are easy to remember and apply.

---

# Artefact Generation Standard

Artefacts should preserve the meaning of the canonical knowledge asset.

Generated artefacts should:

- follow brand standards
- preserve terminology
- maintain hierarchy
- adapt format without changing meaning

Artefacts should not introduce new knowledge.

If new knowledge appears, update the source asset first.

---

# Review Checklist

Before publishing or promoting an asset:

## Purpose

Is the objective clear?

## Structure

Is the logic easy to follow?

## Quality

Is the reasoning sound?

## Reuse

Can this generate future value?

## Consistency

Does it follow Mentat standards?

## Maintenance

Can it evolve over time?

---

# Definition of a Mature Asset

A mature Mentat asset:

- has a clear purpose
- is structured
- is connected
- has been refined
- generates multiple uses
- remains maintainable

---

# Quality Principle

The success of Mentat is not measured by the amount of content produced.
It is measured by the quality and reuse of knowledge accumulated.
Build knowledge that compounds.
